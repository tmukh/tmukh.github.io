Time to Check in on my Japanese Progress!

To preface this series of posts for the next.. I don’t know.. 4-5 months? I’ll be doing an update once a week, reviewing my Anki card progress, that is, how many cards I know and how much my vocabulary has grown, alongside my WaniKani Progress, which is my Kanji learning method.

Well then.. 行こう!

For starters, I am currently on level 4 on WaniKani, which will cover about 30% of the vocab found on sites such as NHK Easy which have simplified Japanese news articles, aimed at aiding those still learning Japanese.

What do the level in WaniKani mean?#

WaniKani has split Kanji groups into different levels, ranked per difficulty, frequency, and scenarios.

I am aiming for around 90% comprehension, for a first milestone, which is around level 24, so on average, that will take 25-30 weeks.

The levels also cover the amount of Kanji needed for each of the JLPT levels. The following is how much the first 27 levels cover

Where am I at?#

Anki Progress#

My current status in Anki shows that I have about 300 words learned/in progress, out of 11,000ish. That is still a ways way away, but I firmly believe I can get this number within 1-2 years.

A mature word is one I’ve gotten right a lot, and is basically “memorized”, learning words are ones I’ve reviewed within the past week or two, which aren’t matured yet.

WaniKani Progress#

As mentioned, I am currently on level 4 on WaniKani with 89 Kanjis learned and 173 words. Seems like my retention rate is floating around 90% which is not too bad (for my memory standards)

Final remarks#

I think it’s important to enjoy the game-ification aspect of WaniKani, but to remember not to speed through it. The faster you try to learn the words, without reviewing the previous ones correctly , the more words will stack up for reviewing. even now I’m at 100 reviews per day! Imagine once I reach level 20-30, those might be up to 1000 reviews if I don’t consistently review and review correctly

Thanks for reading, see you at the next language-learning check-in!#
